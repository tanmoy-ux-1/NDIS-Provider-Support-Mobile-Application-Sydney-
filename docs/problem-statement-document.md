# Problem Statement Document

**Project Title:** NDIS Provider Support Mobile Application (Sydney)

## 1. Problem Definition

NDIS providers in Sydney face significant operational challenges in managing client information, scheduling services, maintaining communication, and ensuring compliance with National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) regulations.
Currently, many providers rely on fragmented systems such as spreadsheets, paper records, and multiple disconnected software tools. This leads to:
- Inefficient workflows
- Increased administrative burden
- Higher risk of errors in client data and reporting
- Difficulty meeting strict compliance requirements
As a result, providers spend more time on administration and less on delivering quality care to participants.

## 2. Target Users

### Primary Users

- NDIS Service Providers (small to medium organisations in Sydney)
- Support Coordinators
- Care Workers / Support Staff

### Secondary Users

- NDIS Participants (Clients)
- Administrative Staff
- Regulatory Bodies (indirect stakeholders)
-Regulatory Bodies (indirect stakeholders)

## 3. User Pain Points

Key issues experienced by users include:
- **Disorganised Client Data**  
  Client information is stored across multiple systems, making it hard to access and update in real time.
- **Inefficient Scheduling**  
  Manual booking processes lead to missed appointments, double bookings, or scheduling conflicts.
- **Communication Gaps**  
  Lack of integrated communication tools results in delays and misunderstandings between staff and clients.
- **Compliance Complexity**  
  Providers must comply with strict reporting and documentation requirements, which are time-consuming and prone to human error.
- **Limited Mobility**  
  Many tools are not mobile-friendly, making it difficult for support workers in the field.

## 4. Australian Market Context

The problem is especially important in Australia due to the scale and structure of the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS).

Key contextual factors:

- **Rapid Growth of NDIS**  
  The NDIS supports over 500,000 Australians, increasing demand for efficient service delivery systems.
- **Strict Compliance Requirements**  
  Providers must meet regulations set by the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission, including detailed reporting and audit readiness.
- **Fragmented Provider Landscape**  
  Many providers in Sydney are small businesses with limited access to enterprise-grade software.
- **Digital Transformation Gap**  
  While healthcare is digitising, many disability service providers still lack integrated, user-friendly mobile solutions.

## 5. Why This Problem Matters

- Improves quality of care by reducing administrative burden
- Enhances compliance and reduces risk
- Increases operational efficiency
- Supports workforce mobility
- Scales with industry growth

## 6. Project Vision

To design and develop a comprehensive, mobile-first application that enables NDIS providers in Sydney to:

- Efficiently manage client data in a centralised system
- Schedule and track service bookings seamlessly
- Communicate effectively with clients and staff
- Automate compliance and reporting processes
- Operate effectively in both office and field environments


## 7. Problem Statement (Final Summary)

NDIS providers in Sydney struggle with fragmented systems for managing client interactions, scheduling, communication, and compliance, leading to inefficiencies, increased administrative burden, and risk of regulatory non-compliance. A unified mobile solution is needed to streamline operations, improve service delivery, and support providers in meeting the growing demands of the Australian disability support sector.
