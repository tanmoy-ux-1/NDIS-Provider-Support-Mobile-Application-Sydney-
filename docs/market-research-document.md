# NDIS Provider Mobile Application Market Research Report Sydney

---

## 1 Market Overview

The NDIS National Disability Insurance Scheme in Australia supports a large number of participants and service providers. In Sydney, many NDIS providers are small to medium organisations that deliver support services to people with disabilities.

Currently, most providers use manual systems such as spreadsheets, paper records, or multiple disconnected software tools. This creates inefficiencies, data errors, and difficulty in managing services effectively.

There is a growing need for a simple mobile friendly and integrated system that helps providers manage their operations in one place.

---

## 2 Top Competitors

The main competitors in the NDIS software market are:

### 1 Careview Advantage  
Careview Advantage provides client management, billing, and compliance reporting for NDIS providers.

### 2 ShiftCare  
ShiftCare offers scheduling, rostering, and mobile workforce management tools.

### 3 Lumary  
Lumary provides advanced reporting, billing, and healthcare support features.

### 4 Better Caring  
Better Caring connects participants with support workers and supports service booking.

### 5 MYP NDIS  
MYP NDIS provides simple plan management and service tracking tools for smaller providers.

---

## 3 Features Offered by Existing Systems

Most existing NDIS platforms provide the following features:

- Client management  
- Service booking and scheduling  
- Billing and payment processing  
- Staff management  
- Reporting and compliance tracking  

These systems mainly focus on administrative and operational tasks for providers.

---

## 4 Market Problems

Despite existing solutions there are still several problems in the market:

1 Many systems are difficult to use and not user friendly  
2 Limited accessibility features for users with disabilities  
3 Poor mobile experience in some platforms  
4 Providers need to use multiple systems instead of one unified platform  
5 Limited real time communication between participants and providers  

---

## 5 Market Opportunity

There is a clear opportunity to develop a simple mobile first NDIS application that solves these issues by:

- Combining all features into one system  
- Improving accessibility for users with disabilities  
- Providing a better mobile experience  
- Reducing administrative workload  
- Improving communication between users and providers  

---

## 6 Conclusion

The NDIS software market in Australia is competitive but still has gaps in usability, accessibility, and mobile functionality. Existing systems focus mainly on administration while user experience is often complex.

This creates an opportunity for a new simple and accessible mobile application that improves efficiency and supports better service delivery for NDIS providers in Sydney.
